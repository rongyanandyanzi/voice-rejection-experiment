## Shared Background Information

Today is a full working day. At the end of the shift, Alex, Lisa, and John check the entrance records and compare them with their observations at the gate. They find that today’s attendance is consistent with the park’s broader seasonal pattern.

Today is a typical off-season weekday, and the park receives only around 500 visitors. The entrance is often quiet for long periods, and staff members at the gate have relatively little work to do.

After reviewing today’s ticket categories and observing visitors at the entrance, they also find that most visitors are families with young children. Many children appear to be preschool or lower-primary-school age.

The entrance data show that families with children under 10 account for around 70%–75% of daily visitors. Other types of visitors make up only a much smaller share.

## Visitor Comments

As ticket-checking employees, Alex, Lisa, and John sometimes hear visitors’ comments while they enter the park. Many parents mention that the park is far away or difficult to reach.

Common parent comments include:

- “It took us almost two hours to get here.”
- “There’s nothing else nearby, so we had to plan the whole day around this trip.”
- “It would be hard to come here often because the location is not very convenient.”
- “The kids like it, but it’s a bit too far for a regular weekend visit.”

They also hear comments from university students:

- “This place is kind of cute, but it feels like it’s only designed for little kids.”
- “If there were more student discounts, I think more people from our university would come.”
- “We came here mainly to take photos, but there aren’t many spots designed for students.”
